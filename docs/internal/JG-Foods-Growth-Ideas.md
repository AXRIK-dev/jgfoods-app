# Where could JG Foods go next?

### A few ideas to talk through — Jon Green & Phil Munro

*These are conversation starters, not a plan set in stone. The point is to understand what growth looks like for you and build towards it.*

---

## More customers finding you

Right now, most of your customers find JG Foods through Facebook or word of mouth. That's great — but it means you're limited to people who already know you're there.

A website that shows up in Google searches — "meat delivery Ormskirk", "mobile butcher near me", "fresh meat West Lancashire" — puts you in front of people who are actively looking for exactly what you offer, but don't know you yet.

**The question for Jon:** Are there areas or types of customer you'd like to reach more of?

---

## Customers who order every week, automatically

Some of your customers probably order most weeks anyway. What if their order just went in automatically — and they got a message giving them the chance to change or skip it before the cut-off?

No chasing. No "same as usual please" messages. The order's already in.

**The question for Jon:** Which of your regular customers would suit this? Domestic, trade, or both?

---

## Word of mouth, with a nudge

Your best customers are already recommending you to people they know. What if they got a small reward for doing it — say, a credit towards their next order when someone they referred places their first?

It doesn't cost anything unless it works, and it turns your most loyal customers into part of the growth.

**The question for Jon:** Would that fit with how you like to run the business?

---

## Growing your trade side

Pubs, restaurants and cafés represent some of your biggest and most reliable orders. Are there more out there you'd like to be supplying?

A dedicated page on the website aimed at trade buyers — written for a kitchen manager or pub landlord, not a family looking for a BBQ pack — could bring enquiries in without you having to knock on doors.

**The question for Jon:** How many trade clients do you have now, and what would the right number look like?

---

## Seasonal opportunities

Christmas turkeys. Summer BBQ packs. Father's Day steak deals. These are moments when people are actively looking to spend on quality meat — and a bit of forward planning can turn them into a reliable bump in revenue each year.

Pre-orders for Christmas, for example, let you know your numbers well in advance and lock customers in early.

**The question for Jon:** Are there times of year that are already busier — and are there any you feel you're missing out on?

---

## If the round keeps growing — a second pair of hands

If things go well, there might come a point where one van and one driver can't cover the demand. The system is built to handle a second driver from day one — separate login, their own run, their own delivery zone.

That's not something to think about now. But it's worth knowing the door is open.

**The question for Jon:** Is that something you'd ever want to explore?

---

*None of this needs to happen straight away — or at all, if it doesn't fit. The core build handles today's business. These are just doors we can open when you're ready.*
