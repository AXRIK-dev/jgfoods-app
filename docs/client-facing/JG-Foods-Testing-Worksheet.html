Try it out — your testing worksheet

A simple walk-through of your new website and admin system. Take your time over the next few days.

Hi Jon — this is your playground. Nothing here is live to real customers yet, so click everything, place pretend orders, make mistakes — you can't break it. Work through each section and tick the box as you go. Jot any notes or anything that feels off, and send them over.

You'll need: your website address, your admin address (admin.jgfoodsnorthwest.com) and the email & password Phil set up for you. When you're done testing, the last section shows you how to wipe all your practice data so you start clean on day one.

1The website (what your customers see)

Open your website on your phone and on a computer if you can — it should look good on both.

Look around the homepage.
Check the wording reads right — including "North Liverpool, Ormskirk & West Lancashire" and the "refer a friend, free pack of sausages or bacon" banner.
Read the "About" section.
Have a proper read of the About part — your story and who you are. Does it sound right and like you? Let me know if it's spot on, needs a few tweaks, or you'd rather we rewrote it from scratch.
📸 A favour: a good photo of you next to the van would be brilliant for the website — it puts a face to the name, and customers really warm to that. Send one over whenever you can.
Browse this week's products.
These come straight from your Availability list in the admin. If it's empty, that's because you haven't added products yet (you'll do that in section 2).
Place a pretend order.
Add a few items, go to checkout, fill in a name, your own phone/email and an address, pick a delivery day, and submit. Use your own details so you can see the order come through.
Check it arrived in the admin.
Open the admin → Delivery Runs. Your pretend order should be there on the day you chose.
Try the contact form.
Scroll to the bottom (Contact) and use the "Send Jon a message" form. Fill it in and send. You should get the message come through to your email — that's a customer enquiry landing with you automatically.

2Logging in & your products

Log in to the admin.
Go to admin.jgfoodsnorthwest.com and sign in with your email and password.
Add a few real products.
Go to Availability → + Add product. Add a name, category, price and switch it Live. Add a photo if you like. These show on the website and in the order/invoice dropdowns.
Edit a price.
Tap a price to change it. Turn a product off with the Live toggle and check it disappears from the website.

3Taking orders

Log an order by hand.
Log Order → choose Enter manually. Type a customer name, phone, address, pick the items and a delivery day, then Save order.
Try the "paste a message" magic.
Log Order → 📋 Paste a message. Paste a pretend WhatsApp order like "Hi Jon, can I get 2x chicken fillets and a BBQ pack for Monday please? It's Dave, 9 Elm Close, 07700 000000" and press Extract details. It should fill the form in for you. Always check it before saving.
📌 Good to know: every order you save automatically creates a receipt (home customers) or an invoice (trade customers) for you — you'll see it in the next section. You don't have to write them out.

4Receipts & invoices

Find the receipt from your order.
Go to Invoices. The order you just logged should have created a receipt/invoice in the Saved invoices & receipts list. (Refresh the page if it's not showing yet.)
Open it as a PDF.
On that row, Actions → Download PDF. Check it looks smart with your JG Foods header, the items and your bank details.
Send it on WhatsApp.
Actions → Send via WhatsApp. WhatsApp opens with the message and a link to the PDF already written — to your own number if you used it. Tap the link to check the PDF opens.
Send it by email.
Actions → Send via email. Your Gmail opens with everything filled in. Send it to yourself and check it arrives.
Build one by hand & log a payment.
At the top of the Invoices page, pick a customer, add a couple of items, Generate then Save invoice. Then on an unpaid one, press Log payment and mark it paid.

5Your delivery days & runs

Set your usual days.
Schedule → turn on the days you normally deliver and Save. Try taking a day off, and adding a one-off day.
Look at a delivery run.
Delivery Runs → tap a day. You'll see the orders, a pick list, and quick temperature entry.
Plan the route.
Press Plan route, then Open in Google Maps — it should open Maps with your stops for real directions.

6Money & your spreadsheets

Enter a day's takings.
Finance → Daily Sales → + Add day. Put in a date, bank and cash amount. It saves on its own. Refresh the page and check it's still there.
Import an old spreadsheet (optional).
On the Daily Sales tab, drag one of your past takings spreadsheets onto the Import past takings box (it needs Date, Bank and Cash columns). It'll read your figures straight in. Do this whenever you're ready — no rush.
Have a look at the other tabs.
Weekly, Bank Payments and Monthly (for your accountant). Check the top figures (Paid, Unpaid, Overdue) look sensible as you add invoices and payments.

7Social posts & VIPs

Write a Facebook/Instagram post.
Social → jot a few notes, press Generate post, tweak it, then Copy and paste into Facebook or Instagram.
Message a VIP.
VIP → write a message (use [Name] and it fills in each person's name), then tap 💬 WhatsApp next to a customer to send it.

8Temperatures (food safety)

Log a temperature.
Temps → tap a cell and enter a fridge temperature. You can also do this from a delivery run while you're out.

9Managing logins (you're the boss)

Open your account page.
Tap your name in the top-right corner.
Change your own password.
Under My password, type a new one twice and update it. Try logging out and back in with it.
Add a login for your driver.
Under Add a login, enter their email and a password, choose Driver (deliveries only — no money), and Create. Tell them the details so they can sign in. You can reset their password or remove them any time.

★Before you go live — wipe your practice data

Once you're happy and ready for real customers, clear out everything you added while testing so you start with a clean, accurate system. Do it in this order (it matters):

⚠️ Important: delete things in the order below. The system won't let you delete a customer who still has invoices or orders attached — so invoices first, then orders, then the customer last.
  1. Invoices & receipts — Invoices page → on each test one, Actions → Delete invoice.
  2. Orders — Delivery Runs → open each test day → open each test order → Delete.
  3. Daily takings — Finance → Daily Sales → press the × on any practice rows (leave the real imported ones if you want to keep them).
  4. Customers — Customers page → open each test customer → Delete.
  5. Products — Availability → remove any made-up test products (keep your real range).
  6. VIPs — VIP page → Remove any test entries.
  7. Extra logins — your name (top-right) → remove any test driver logins you don't need. (Keep your own!)
When you're done, the Dashboard and Finance figures should read back to zero / empty. That's your sign you're starting fresh. From that point on, every real order builds your real records.
